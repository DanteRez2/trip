International airport recommendations

Arrival: Tokyo Haneda (HND)

Best choice. Closest major international airport to central Tokyo, with easy Keikyu / Tokyo Monorail access.

Best for Ginza, Tokyo Station, Shinagawa, Asakusa or Ueno areas.

Alternative: Tokyo Narita (NRT)

More distant but sometimes cheaper. Consider only if the flight is meaningfully better.

Plan roughly 60–90 minutes into central Tokyo.

Departure: Kansai International (KIX)

Best international departure airport from Kyoto. Direct JR Haruka train from Kyoto Station.

Avoids returning to Tokyo just to fly home.

Alternative: Osaka Itami (ITM)

Closer to Kyoto/Osaka but mostly domestic; not ideal for Europe unless paired with a domestic connection.

HND → Tokyo

Keikyu is often best for Shinagawa/Ginza/Asakusa sides. Tokyo Monorail is simple for Hamamatsucho and JR connections.

Budget: €3–6/person.

NRT → Tokyo

Use Narita Express for Tokyo/Shinjuku/Shibuya or Skyliner for Ueno/Nippori.

Budget: €15–25/person.

Tokyo → Kyoto

Tokaido Shinkansen from Tokyo/Shinagawa to Kyoto. Reserved seats usually go on sale one month ahead.

Budget: approx €85/person.

Kyoto → Nara

Kintetsu is often most convenient for Nara Park; JR works well from Kyoto Station.

Budget: approx €8–12/person return.

Kyoto → KIX

JR Haruka Express direct from Kyoto Station to Kansai Airport.

Budget: approx €20–25/person.

JR Pass?

Do not buy the national JR Pass for this route. Point-to-point tickets are cheaper for one Tokyo→Kyoto Shinkansen plus local trains.

Weather, foliage and strategy

Early November is excellent for walking: generally mild, less humid and more comfortable than summer.

Temperature expectation: Tokyo and Kyoto often sit roughly around 10–18°C in early/mid November. Bring layers, a light jacket and comfortable shoes.

Autumn colors: Kyoto’s peak foliage is often later in November, so this may be early for full red-maple intensity. The upside is slightly less peak-season pressure than late November.

Routing strategy: the planner intentionally groups nearby places, avoids cross-city zigzags, and puts lower-effort blocks after high-effort attractions.
